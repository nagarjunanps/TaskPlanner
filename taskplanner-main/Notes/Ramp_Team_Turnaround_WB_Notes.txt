Ramp Operations – Wide Body (WB) Flight Flow 

(AirAsia GTR Context) 

Wide Body (WB) ramp operations follow a specialized and structured workflow distinct from Narrow Body (NB) operations. While the overall turnaround concept remains similar, WB handling requires certified manpower, specialized heavy equipment, making it a separate operational stream within Ramp Operations. 

All personnel assigned to WB flights are wide body–certified based on the Foreign Carriers (FOCA) requirement and not interchangeable with NB teams, due to equipment complexity and operational requirements. The total ramp ops processing should be completed with in one hour for the WB turnaround flight.  

 

1. WB Team Composition & Skill Requirements 

Each WB flight is handled by a dedicated and certified team, comprising: 

Role 

Responsibility 

Duty Manager (DM) 

Overall operational oversight 

Ramp Loading Supervisor (RLS) 

Load control, compliance, and supervision 

Driver (GSE Operator) 

Baggage/container towing 

Loader 

Aircraft-side loading and unloading 

JCPL Operator 

Container loader operation 

Key Characteristics 

All personnel must hold WB operational certification 

Each FOCA has different set of compliance rules. Staff must pass the required tests to obtain clearance for handling WB flights. 

Trained to operate:  

Container loaders 

Heavy tow tractors 

WB-specific ground support equipment 

No workforce sharing with Narrow Body operations 

 

 

2. Equipment & Operational Differences (WB vs NB) 

2.1 WB-Specific Equipment 

WB operations require heavier and specialized Ground Support Equipment (GSE), including: 

Container loaders (operated by JCPL) 

Heavy-duty tow tractors 

WB-compatible baggage and cargo handling systems 

2.2 Pushback Operations 

Pushback is required for both NB and WB flights 

WB pushback uses heavy-duty, aircraft-specific tow equipment 

Equipment differs significantly due to aircraft weight and size 

2.3 Cargo Door Access – Structural Difference 

NB Operations  

Cargo doors accessible directly from ground level 

WB Operations  

Cargo doors not reachable from ground 

Passenger Steps (PAX steps) are mandatory for:  

Opening cargo doors 

Closing cargo doors 
 

3. Additional Arrival Data Inputs (WB-Specific) 

Wide Body arrivals receive additional documentation from the origin station (X station): 

LDM – Load Message 

CPM – Container / Pallet Message 

These documents are used for: 

Load distribution validation 

Container position verification 

Arrival handling coordination 

 

4. WB Arrival Flight Process 

4.1 Pre-Arrival Preparation 

Entire WB team reports T–15 minutes along with the required equipment 

GSE positioned and safety-checked 

Operational readiness confirmed by RLS 

4.2 Aircraft Arrival & Securing 

Aircraft lands and parks at assigned bay 

Wheel chocks placed by loaders 

Aircraft alignment via:  

VDGS, or 

Manual marshalling (if required) 

4.3 Cargo Door Access 

WB cargo doors are not ground-accessible 

PAX steps are positioned for:  

Cargo door opening 

Mandatory step before unloading 

4.4 Passenger Bridge Connection 

Passenger Boarding Bridge (PBB):  

Connected by airport authority staff 

Not handled by ramp team. RLS will document the timing of the PLB docking and retracted time. 

In case of faster operations, upon requesting airline, two PLB can be connected. 

4.5 Arrival Data Verification 

RLS verifies:  

LDM 

CPM 

 

Ensures alignment between:  

Actual aircraft load 

Planned container configuration 

Ground handling sequence 

4.6 Unloading Operations 

Passenger baggage unloading begins 

Driver transports baggage to baggage belt 

Cargo unloading conducted in parallel:  

Containers handled using JCPL equipment 

Cargo transported to warehouse for processing (Bay to staging area and then finally towed to Warehouse) 

 

5. WB Departure Flight Process 

Departure activities begin only after complete unloading and turnaround clearance. 

5.1 Load Plan Preparation 

AirAsia Load Control prepares official load plan 

Based on:  

Available baggage containers 

Cargo containers 

Load plan requires Captain’s signed approval 

For FOCA / other airlines:  

Load plans received from the external sources (e.g., FM Altea & Sabre system) 

5.2 Load Execution Supervision 

RLS supervises aircraft-side loading 

Ensures:  

Approved sequencing 

Structural compliance 

Safety adherence 

5.3 Container Movement & Loading 

Driver tows containers to aircraft 

JCPL operates container loaders 

Loader positions containers into correct compartments 

5.4 Cargo Door Access for Loading 

PAX steps used again due to cargo door height 

Required for door opening and closing during loading 

5.5 Completion & Verification 

All loading verified by AirAsia staff 

RLS confirms:  

Full load plan compliance 

Operational safety clearance 

5.6 Captain Sign-Off 

Final load confirmation and approval obtained 

Mandatory before pushback clearance 

5.7 Pushback & Departure 

Pushback performed using WB-specific heavy pushback equipment 

Aircraft cleared for departure 

Flight proceeds to take-off 

 

6. Key Summary – WB Operational Characteristics 

Dedicated WB-certified workforce only 

Uses heavy-duty and specialized GSE 

Cargo handling is container-based, not bulk 

PAX steps required for cargo door access 

Receives LDM & CPM from origin station 

Strict load plan compliance with Captain approval 

Pushback uses different equipment than NB operations
