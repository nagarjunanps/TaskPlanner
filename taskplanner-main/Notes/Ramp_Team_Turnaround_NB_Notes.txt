AirAsia – GTR (Ground Team Red) Ramp Operations Workflow Automation Context
 
Context & Objective The Ramp Ops Team meeting reviewed current ground‑handling operations for AirAsia flights at KLIA T2, with the objective of:
 
Reducing manual work and human errors Minimising delays caused by frequent flight changes Automating flight updates, manpower planning, and communication Improving overall operational efficiency, accuracy, and responsiveness
 
The goal is to shift from a manual, reactive process to a system‑driven, near‑real‑time workflow.
 
Scale, Performance & Operational Pressure
 
Daily flight volume: ~255–256 AirAsia turnaround flights per day (around 570 flights including both Arrival & Departures) On‑time performance (OTP):
 
Best months: ~98.9% without any delay Worst months: can fall to ~77%
 
Change intensity:
 
~70–80% of daily AirAsia flights experience schedule, bay, or timing changes Many changes occur close to departure time, sometimes within minutes
 
 
Turnaround constraints:
 
A320: ~30 minutes (Narrow body) A321: ~35 minutes (Narrow body) A330: ~1 hour 45 minutes (Wide body)
 
Extremely tight turnaround windows create high workload, stress, and limited recovery time if errors occur.
 
 
Current Manual Workflow & Data Flow 3.1 Source Data
 
Red Watch application (AirAsia):
 
Daily flight schedule published in AirAsia Redwatch application after 03:00 AM once bays are confirmed Includes STD, ETD, bay, cargo weight
 
Structured AirAsia emails & CSV files:
 
Continuous flight change notifications throughout the day (every few minutes) from AirAsia to GTR team via emails
 
 
3.2 Manual Processing
 
Allocators from command center:
 
Manually download schedules Copy/paste flight data into internal movement and rostering spreadsheets Sheets have no formulas or automation Visual indicators are applied using manual color coding
 
 
3.3 Communication Method
 
Flight and manpower changes shared via WhatsApp, Work vivo
 
100+ WhatsApp groups in use and phone device is too small to accomodate the screenshots posted by the team as part of flight updates Updates sent as screenshots or image clippings of Excel sheets
 
 
This creates:
 
Information overload Version mismatches High risk of missed or misinterpreted updates
 
 
3.4 Continuous Monitoring Requirement
 
Someone must constantly monitor inboxes for flight changes Last‑minute changes often occur after teams have already prepared Bay changes are especially disruptive when not communicated instantly
 
 
Workforce Composition, Roles & Rostering 4.1 Workforce Mix
 
Permanent local GTR staff Vendor / contractor staff (recently onboarded)
 
Require guidance from local staff Have credentialing and system‑access limitations
 
 
4.2 Key Roles
 
DTM (Duty Terminal Manager): Overall command‑center control RLS (Ramp Loading Supervisor): Highest authority below wing Ramp Loaders or Ramp agents (usually teams of 3 referred as 1 Set) Drivers (GSE operators)
 
BLM = Driven TBL = Towed
 
Pushback Operator Headset / VDGS Operator Runners / Support staff
 
4.3 Standard Team Composition (per flight)
 
1 RLS 1 Driver (arrives ~T‑15 mins) 1 Loader team (3 loaders or ramp agents) 1 Tower
 
4.4 Assignment Rules
 
Cargo < 1.5 tons → 1 team Cargo > 1.5 tons or dual‑hold loading → 2 teams Heavy sectors (up to ~10–10.9 tons) → 3 teams The Wide body (A330) operators cannot be assigned to Narrow body (A320, A321) as operating for A330 requires high end machine handling and hence work force cannot be mixed.
 
Turnaround logic:
 
Ground time > 45 min → separate arrival & departure teams Ground time ≤ 45 min → same team handles full turnaround
 
 
4.5 Rostering & Shifts
 
Multiple shift patterns:
 
05:00–15:00 11:00–23:00 14:30–00:30 23:00–11:00
 
 
Rosters managed manually via annual / weekly Excel sheets Adjustments made constantly due to flight changes or delayed flights

user journey
Entire team will be onsite T-15 before the Arrival flight lands, they will do initial prep activity
Once flight arrives
Loader will keep on chocks
VDGS or manual marshalling will be done by the Ramp agents
PLB bridge will be connected by the Airport authority staff (not done by Ramp ops team), RLS to note down the time
Arrival unloading starts, loaders will unload the Arrival bags and driver will tow them to the baggage belt area
Cargo unloading happens in parallel with baggage unloading and cargo will be taken to the warehouse
The departure process will start once all unloading is done
Baggage and Cargo loading start as part of departure flight
The Airasia staff will be monitoring the entire process and they will verify if all the activities are completed
RLS will be overseeing the entire ops on ground.
once all the process is completed, RLS will get signature from captain.
Then pushback of the flight happens.
Flight is ready to take off.
 
Operational Rules & Procedures
 
Teams must be ready 15 minutes before arrival for group prep VDGS and equipment must be set before aircraft arrival
 
If RLS is unavailable, runners operate VDGS or use manual marshalling
 
 
Color coding used on spreadsheets:
 
A / B / STD / ETD changes Orange for special requests (e.g., two‑door boarding)
 
 
Passenger Boarding Bridge (PBB):
 
Normally 1 bridge For short turnarounds (< 1 hour, often 30–40 mins), two bridges used
 
 
 
Key Pain Points & Failure Modes
 
Manual copy‑paste causes latency and frequent human errors Color‑based interpretation is subjective and error‑prone WhatsApp communication:
 
Too noisy Easy to miss updates Screenshots increase risk of misreading data
 
 
Vendor staff:
 
Lack system IDs and email integration Not fully visible in rostering tools
 
 
Manual rostering cannot keep up with the speed and frequency of changes Screenshots of Excel sheets in WhatsApp create high operational risk Staffing optimisation based on real‑time flight changes is nearly impossible manually Dynamic allocation of staff is not possible. If the staff goes on medical leave, the rostering has to be corrected at the last moment or the team will work without that person Not able to monitor the staff has actually reported to the location. DTM from command center cannot concentrate on the problematic flight instead DTM is checking on all the flights. Productivity of the staff is not monitored.
