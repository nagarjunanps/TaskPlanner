Meeting Transcript (Organized Version)
 
1. Counter Segregation
- Counters are divided by domestic vs. international flights, not by individual flight sectors.
- Domestic flights: Counters S and T.
- International flights: Counters U through Y.
- This segregation is displayed on a giant screen for passengers.
- Example:
- Domestic passenger → Check-in at S/T.
- International passenger → Check-in at U–Y.
 
---
 
2. Rostering and Deployment
- Operations are generally easier compared to rostering and planning, which are more complex.
- Ramp operations also face challenges in rostering.
- Current system in use: GES (staff allocation system).
- Staff perform operations via the application.
 
---
 
3. Online Check-in Process
- Online check-in is enforced for Asia flights.
- Current online check-in rate: 70–80%.
- Process:
- Notification sent 14 days before flight.
- Online check-in available until 1 hour before departure.
- Counters also close 1 hour before departure.
- Charges:
- Self check-in: Free.
- Assisted check-in: 30 Ringgit per passenger (boarding pass fee).
- Passenger behavior:
- Some passengers still prefer assisted check-in despite the fee.
- Types of counters:
- Baggage drop counters.
- Assisted check-in counters.
 
---
 
4. Staffing at Check-in Pillars
- Airlines require staff presence at four pillars (main congestion points).
- Four staff members are deployed at each pillar.
- Purpose:
- Manage congestion.
- Assist passengers unfamiliar with self check-in.
- Intervention only when passengers cannot self check-in.
- Frequent flyers usually manage independently.
 
---
 
5. Baggage Tagging
- Passengers can print baggage tags during self check-in.
- System allows printing up to 10 tags, even if fewer bags are checked in.
- Comparison between tags printed at kiosks vs. open-back systems was mentioned (details to be shared separately).
 
---
 
6. Ground Operations & Shifts
- Responsibilities include:
- Counter operations.
- Transfer desk operations (connections between origin, transit, and destination).
- Gate operations.
- Special handling passengers (wheelchair, visually impaired, etc.).
- Shift structure:
- Morning: 2 AM, 3 AM, 5 AM → until 12 PM.
- Afternoon: 11 AM, 2 PM, 4:30 PM → transition until evening.
- Rostering manager (Zul) will provide further details in a Friday session.
 
---
 
7. Interaction with Flight Crew
- After process completion, checklists are handed over to the Captain.
- Airlines (e.g., AirAsia Philippines, AirAsia Thailand) require individual trip records for each flight.
- Compliance is critical for audits; failure results in NCR (Non-Compliance Report).
 
---
 
8. Counter Closure & Pre-Breakup
- Counters close 1 hour before scheduled departure (STD).
- Example: STD = 10:00 → Counters close at 9:00.
- Pre-breakup figures sent to Flight Ops.
- Final figures updated at the gate, accounting for no-shows.
 
---
 
9. No-Show Handling
- No-shows identified 20 minutes before departure (morning flights) or 10 minutes before departure (later flights).
- Process:
- Initiate NX17 via Work Vivo platform.
- Ramp team searches for baggage linked to no-show passengers.
- Updates shared in system (similar to comment threads).
- Systems used:
- GoNow system for passenger and baggage data.
- Boarding pass scanning (above wing).
- Baggage tag scanning (ramp side).
- Final reports include:
- GD (General Declaration) for aircraft.
- POB (Passengers on Board).
- Baggage count.
- All reported to Captain before departure.
